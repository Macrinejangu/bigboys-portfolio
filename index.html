Big Boys of Shibale

A captivating dramatized storytelling experience

About Big Boys of Shibale

"Big Boys of Shibale" is a captivating dramatized storytelling performance featuring seasoned stage performers Mark Wabwire and Allan Wasike ("Emperor Shaker"). The show features two narrators (storytellers) supported by a live band playing a blend of modern and traditional instruments. All band members contribute vocals, and dancers enhance the delivery at various stages of the performance.

The performance has been staged on various platforms including Kenya National Theatre (KNT), Kenya International Theatre Festival (KITFEST 2023 & 2024), and the Kampala International Theatre Festival (KITF 2024). It was nominated for Best Storytelling Production at the 2023 Kenya Theatre Awards and won Best Sound Design in the 2024 edition.

Synopsis

Set in Shibale, a township in western Kenya, the story unfolds when the region's primary economic lifeline, the Mumias Sugar Factory, collapses. Four young men—Lalo Korongo, Juma Ngangari, Saleh Chonjo, and Kevo Bright—must overcome their childhood differences and find ways to survive the ensuing economic disaster.

Their gains are legendary, but new conflicts emerge as money, success, and the thirst for power fuel enmity. As tensions rise, their struggles affect not only their lives but also the community and the region’s economy. "Big Boys of Shibale" explores their ventures, the societal impact of their actions, and the new economic landscape they influence.

About the Tour

"The Big Boys of Shibale" will embark on a dynamic and vibrant theatre tour across five key counties in Kenya: Nairobi, Mombasa, Eldoret, Kisumu, and Kakamega. This exciting tour has been made possible by the prestigious HII STAGE II grant, awarded by the French government through the French Embassy in Kenya, Creation Africa, and administered by Alliance Française Nairobi.

The performance was selected for the grant due to its artistic excellence, significant impact on the Kenyan theatre scene, and proven track record of success. The HII STAGE II grant celebrates the immense talent and creativity within Kenya’s artistic community, highlighting the performance's viability and vibrancy. This tour represents a significant milestone for Kenya’s creative industry, showcasing Kenyan theatre on a larger stage, creating employment, fostering new collaborations, and unlocking endless opportunities within the arts and culture sector.
